01 / Background

About

Dr. Rahman is an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science and Mathematics at Algoma University, Brampton, ON, Canada. He also holds an adjunct assistant professor position at the School of Computer Science at the University of Windsor, ON, Canada, and a research associate position at the NORDIK Institute in Sault Ste. Marie, ON, Canada.

He obtained his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Windsor in 2020, and completed a second master's degree in Computer Science from the University of Lethbridge, Alberta, Canada. He obtained his master's and bachelor's degrees in Computer Science and Engineering from Jahangirnagar University, Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Dr. Rahman has also worked as a Software Engineer in Bangladesh and Canada for three years prior to entering academia full-time.

Prospective MSc COSC Students: Prospective Master of Science (MSc COSC) students are requested to contact Dr. Rahman at zamilur.rahman AT algomau DOT ca to inquire about open positions. Replies with interview details will be sent if you are considered for a position.

03 / Focus Areas

Research Interests

Graph Theory Complex Network Analysis Data-Driven Analysis Mental Health Applications Economics & Decision Sciences Machine Learning Natural Language Processing

Dr. Rahman's research interests include graph theory and its applications, complex network analysis, and data-driven analysis, with a focus on applications in mental health, economics, and decision sciences. He also explores the use of machine learning and natural language processing in these domains.
